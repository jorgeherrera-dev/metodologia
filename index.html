What was the Cold War?

The Cold War was a period of political, economic, and military tension between the United States and the Soviet Union that lasted from 1947 to 1991. It was called "cold" because there was never direct military confrontation between the two superpowers.

The 1950s were crucial in defining the rules of the game: military alliances were established, the nuclear arms race began, and the world became divided into two opposing ideological blocs.
